Confidentiality

Due to the sensitive nature of Petronas' internal operations, specific project names, budgets, and technical architectures discussed during this interview are omitted to comply with Non-Disclosure protocols.

The Objective

Academic study provides the framework, but industry exposure provides the context. I initiated this interview with a Project Management Office (PMO) Executive at Petronas to understand how large-scale IT projects are managed in a high-stakes corporate environment.

The focus was to deconstruct the lifecycle of a real-world IT implementation, moving beyond textbook definitions of "Agile" and "Waterfall" to understand the hybrid realities of the oil and gas sector.

From Interview to Application

1. Requirement Gathering (The Interview)

Conducted a structured interview to extract data on project timelines, resource allocation challenges, and risk mitigation strategies used within Petronas.

2. Synthesis & Reporting

Compiled the raw interview data into a comprehensive industry analysis report. This document highlighted the contrast between theoretical "Critical Path Method" and the actual adaptive planning used by the executive.

3. MS Project Simulation

To validate my understanding, I reconstructed the project's timeline using Microsoft Project. I generated a detailed Gantt Chart based on the phases described by the executive, effectively translating verbal insights into a technical project schedule.
